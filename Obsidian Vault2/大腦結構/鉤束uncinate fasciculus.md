### Uncinate fasciculus

The [uncinate fasciculus](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/neuroscience/uncinate-fasciculus "Learn more about uncinate fasciculus from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages") is a fiber bundle connecting the anterior part of the temporal lobe and the orbitofrontal area (Figs. 14 and 15).24 The functions of this bundle are poorly understood.31 Uncinate fasciculus is considered to belong to the [limbic system](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/neuroscience/limbic-system "Learn more about limbic system from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages") and could be involved in emotion processing and memory.81 It may also play an important role in lexical retrieval, semantic associations, and aspects of naming that require connections from temporal to frontal components of the language network (eg, the naming of actions).70 Likewise the [ILF](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/neuroscience/inferior-longitudinal-fasciculus "Learn more about ILF from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages"), a subcortical electrical stimulation study also suggested that uncinate fasciculus is not essential for language.
